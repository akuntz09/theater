ACTOR 2: brutal question, almost. by definition, improv fits into the post-modern school along with elements of satire and humor. it began in the late 50's with miles davis in jazz music and the same is true with improv in dance and theater. improv on one level is simply a tool to generate material that will later by shaped into a work. i think that many find that the doing of improv is so much fun that they want to take it to the stage ~ which can be incredible and can also appear indulgent and narcissistic. since it is, indeed, improvised one takes chances whether any given night will be any good needing both the performer and the audience to be receptive and willing to connect. in the context of performance art - i have no problem at all thinking of improv as an art form.
